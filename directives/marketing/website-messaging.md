# Website Messaging Framework

---

## Core Larsen Principles Applied

### 1. Messaging should resonate with your ICP — AND ONLY your ICP

From "How To Attract High Quality Leads": "not only should it resonate with your ICP, it should ONLY resonate with your ICP." The language itself is a filter. If someone below the ICP reads the site and thinks "this isn't for me," the messaging is working.

### 2. Language level = client level

Low-level language attracts low-level clients. High-level language attracts high-level clients. Even if the service is the same, the copy determines who responds.

**Larsen example — roofing:**
- Low-level: "Are you sick of cold calling?" → solo operators
- High-level: "Does your sales team need more volume?" → multi-million dollar companies

**Wraith equivalent:**
- Low-level (AVOID): "Grow your social media" / "Get more followers" / "We'll handle your marketing"
- High-level (USE): "Build distribution without losing engineering velocity" / "Compounding content pipeline" / "Founder-led video across every platform"

### 3. Plain English over clever copy

"Sometimes the best copy to sell a horse is horses for sale." State what it is. Quantify it. 3 lines or less for the headline. The sub-headline explains how.

### 4. Price on display

"This is 2025 — people who hide the price, there's really no value in doing that anymore." Show the price. It filters. Someone who can't afford $1,200/mo won't waste your time.

### 5. Disqualification is a feature

The form should prevent unqualified people from booking a call. This keeps every data point clean and every call productive.

## Language Rules

### USE — Only Alex (the ICP) uses these terms:
- "Founder-led content" — category term only founders in content-aware circles use
- "Distribution" — funded founder language (not "marketing")
- "Engineering velocity" / "engineering time" — only someone with an engineering team says this
- "Content pipeline" — systems thinking language
- "Pre-Series A" / "runway" — fundraising language
- "Product Hunt launch" — startup ecosystem
- "RAG" / "inference costs" / "fine-tuning" — technical AI depth signal
- "Compounding" — asymmetric bet / systems thinker language
- "Pipeline" (sales) — B2B language
- "NPS" / "traction" — product-market fit language

### NEVER USE — People below the ICP use these terms:
- "Social media management" — generic small business
- "Grow your following" — influencer / creator
- "Build your brand" — everyone says this, means nothing
- "Content creation services" — commodity
- "Affordable" — signals cheap, attracts cheap
- "Go viral" — lottery thinking
- "Boost your online presence" — local business
- "Get more likes / followers" — vanity metrics
- "Digital marketing" — too broad
- "We'll handle your social media" — generic agency
- "No experience needed" — beginner
- "Easy" / "Simple" as primary selling points — low-level framing

## Problems, Outcomes, Circumstances by Level

### ICP-Level Problems (USE):
- "Growth has plateaued since our Product Hunt launch"
- "Investors keep asking about our distribution strategy"
- "Our competitors are everywhere online and we're invisible"
- "I keep telling myself I'll post this week and then I don't"
- "I can't explain our tool simply on camera"
- "Hiring a marketer at this stage burns 3–6 months of runway"

### Below-ICP Problems (NEVER USE):
- "I don't have any content"
- "I need help with social media"
- "I don't have followers"
- "I can't afford a marketing person"
- "I want to go viral"
- "I need someone to run my Instagram"

### ICP-Level Outcomes (USE):
- "Build a compounding distribution engine"
- "Get content out the door without context-switching off product"
- "Look legitimate to investors and enterprise buyers"
- "Convert content presence into qualified pipeline"

### Below-ICP Outcomes (NEVER USE):
- "Get my first followers"
- "Start creating content"
- "Learn how to market my product"
- "Get more likes on my posts"

## Page Messaging Architecture

### Index (Homepage)
| Section | Content Strategy |
|---------|-----------------|
| Call-out | "FOR AI TOOL FOUNDERS" — Larsen's "serving realtors" equivalent |
| Headline | Deliverable + specificity: "20 Founder-Led Videos Per Month for Your AI Tool" |
| Sub-headline | Mechanism + time commitment: the pipeline, the platforms, the 2.5 hours |
| Trust badges | Niche signal + time + price: "AI tool companies only" / "2.5 hrs/mo" / "From $1,200/mo" |
| VSSL | Walk through the offer. Plain English. What it is, who it's for, how it works, proof, CTA. |
| Blueprint section | Lead magnet — playbook for AI founders on content distribution |
| FAQ | Top 5 ICP objections from the validated list |
| Bottom CTA | Urgency through reality: "Your competitors shipped content this week. Did you?" |

### Apply (Application Form)
| Element | Principle |
|---------|-----------|
| Multi-step form | Typeform-style, one question per screen |
| Price shown | Q6 asks budget capacity at $1,200–$3,500/mo range — Larsen price transparency |
| DQ questions | Stage (filters pre-launch), budget (filters broke), product description (filters no-product) |
| Calendly embedded | Step 10 — book the call at the end of the form |
| Copy tone | Direct, minimal, no hand-holding |

### Blueprint (Lead Magnet)
| Element | Principle |
|---------|-----------|
| Hero | Playbook title + who it's for + time to read |
| Form | Name, email, company, stage, challenge — captures lead data |
| Fine print | No spam promise — keep it honest |

## Validated Objections (FAQ Source)

From ICP doc section "Common Objections / Limiting Beliefs":

1. "Nobody can explain my product as well as I can."
2. "AI-generated content feels inauthentic and my audience will notice."
3. "I tried posting for a while and it didn't work."
4. "Content agencies don't understand technical products."
5. "$2,000/month is a lot when we're burning cash."
6. "We should focus on product first and marketing later."

Use objections 1–5 on the homepage FAQ. Use #6 in email flows and outreach.

## Edge Cases & Learnings

_Updated as the site goes live and conversations happen._

- Larsen note: when starting out with zero conversions, reduce friction to get ANY qualified people on calls. Don't over-DQ. Get data first, then tighten.
- The "do nothing" competitor is the biggest threat. Bottom CTA should make cost of inaction concrete.
- Bootstrapped vs funded framing: "From $1,200/mo" works for both. Funded founders see it as a budget line item. Profitable bootstrappers see $100/video math.
